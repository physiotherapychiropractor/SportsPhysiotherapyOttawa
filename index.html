What Is Physiotherapy?
Physiotherapy can be defined as the practice of treating injury, disease or other deformities using manual techniques like joint manipulation and massage. A physiotherapist provides advice, guidance, exercise, and manual therapy to people with disabilities, injury or illness.

The key feature of physiotherapy lies in its holistic approach to well-being and treatment. This focuses on the entire person and their general health.

Physiotherapy helps individuals remain independent and as active as possible in society.

Common Physiotherapy Methods

Most physiotherapy techniques are hand-on and designed to mobilize and manipulate the tissue in the body. It helps to reduce pain, stiffness, improve blood circulation, encourage movement and relaxation. These include:

  • Massage can be used to stimulate soft tissue in the body
  • Stretching, myofascial relaxation and other soft tissue techniques are some examples.
  • Joint mobilization is a form of manipulation that uses the glide of the joint for motion, control, and function.
  • The handcrafted instrument used in physiotherapy instrument mobilisation (PIM).
  • The use of minimal energy techniques (METs), which uses muscle's energy to relax muscles by using autogenic and reciprocal inhibition. They also lengthen the muscle.
  • Osteopathy and acupuncture are also available.
The Differences Between the Two

Both physical therapy as well as physiotherapy employ movement and hands on techniques to improve the bodies condition. But they are different in how this is done. As you can see, physiotherapy emphasizes manual therapies while physical therapy more heavily relies on exercise. You can reach the same goals with different types of treatment.

In addition to physical therapy, physiotherapy is used more often in another setting. The majority of physiotherapy services are provided in hospitals. They can be used for post-operative and urgent care. Physical therapy, on the other hand is used more frequently for minor injuries. The primary focus of a physical therapist is to assess and treat problems such as pain, muscle strains, injury, or chronic movement issues, like arthritis.

Read The Evidence

Physiotherapy is a science-based profession and takes a 'whole person' approach to health and wellbeing, which includes the patient's general lifestyle.

At the core is the patient's involvement in their own care, through education, awareness, empowerment and participation in their treatment.

The benefits of physiotherapy can be accessed at any stage in your life. Physiotherapy can help with sudden injuries or back pain, long-term conditions like asthma management, as well as preparing for childbirth and sporting events.

Types

Physical therapy can be done in many different ways, so therapists may choose to focus on one type of treatment. The following types are available:

  • Orthopedic physical therapy: This treats musculoskeletal injuries, involving the muscles, bones, ligaments, fascia, and tendons. You can use it for injuries such as bursitis or tendinitis, fractures and chronic conditions. You can have joint mobilizations, manual therapies, strength training and mobility training for patients.
  • Geriatric physical therapy can be used to treat older people with mobility or physical impairments, such as arthritis, osteoporosis and balance problems, incontinence, and hip and joint replacement. This intervention is designed to improve mobility, decrease pain and enhance physical fitness.
  • People with certain cardiopulmonary conditions or surgical procedures may benefit from cardiovascular rehabilitation. Treatment can increase muscular and cardiovascular endurance and stamina.
  • Wound therapy: It can ensure that the wound heals properly and receives adequate blood supply through increased circulation. You may also use e-stim or compression therapy for your physical therapy.
  • Vestibular treatment: This is used to address balance difficulties that could be caused inner ear conditions. There are a variety of manual exercises that vestibular physical therapists can use to assist patients in regaining their coordination and balance.
  • Decongestive therapy: It can be used to drain fluid accumulated in lymphedema patients and others with fluid-related conditions.
  • Pelvic floor rehabilitation: This can help treat conditions that affect the pelvic floor, such as urinary or fecal incontinence, or urinary urgency and pelvic pain as a result of injuries or surgery or because of certain conditions.

Solution: Understanding the difference between physical therapy and physiotherapy

Physical therapy is often used interchangeably with physiotherapy. That is to say, most people do not make a distinction between the two modalities. A lot of countries worldwide use the term "physiotherapy" to describe this practice. However, the United States uses physical therapy as the predominant term. Physiotherapy, however, can be used either to describe physical therapy, or to indicate a similar therapy. The difference between physical and physiotherapy is minimal.

However, physical therapy can be distinguished from physiotherapy by stating a minor but important difference. Some health care workers believe that physiotherapy can be described as a hands-on method of rehabilitation therapy. The physiotherapist employs manual techniques to assist the patient. Some physiotherapists may also offer deepening stretching or other treatments. Others would argue that physical therapy can be largely exercise-based. It is possible to disagree on this matter but one thing is certain: physical therapy has many advantages for senior citizens.

When is Physiotherapy Useful?

Conditions of the heart or lungs

They run heart rehabilitation centers that educate their patients about quitting smoking and how to live a healthier lifestyle. The physiotherapists also care for patients with chronic obstructive and restrictive pulmonary diseases, chronic bronchitis or emphysema.

The physiotherapists teach patients who have cystic Fibrosis, which is when mucous becomes sticky and thickens and clogs their lungs, about various techniques. This allows them to clean out their lungs from the privacy of their home.

A physiotherapist may recommend an active lifestyle to asthmatic patients. This will include specific exercises and resistance training. This can all help to improve quality of your life.

Conditions neurologic

PT can help patients with neurological disorders such as multiple sclerosis (MS), Parkinson's disease (PD), cerebral palsy and spinal cord injury. A physiotherapist might help to improve motor skills, maintain proper posture, and correct gait with the aid of mechanical devices.

Orthopedic conditions

Orthopedic physiotherapists treat conditions that can affect bone and joints, including trauma. These include back pain, sprains and fractures. Once a surgery is performed, physiotherapists introduce joint mobilization techniques such as acupuncture (dry needling), hot and cold packs, and electrical stimulation.

If there are fractures, PT may be able to help with the re-establishment of movement and stiffness.
